Raising disciples of all nations

Many churchgoers who claim to be Christians today have become complacent in their prayer lives and Bible study habits, leading to the rise of fake ministers misleading half-baked believers into unfathomable errors. Alarmingly, these imposters are welcomed among Christian ministers as brethren in the same faith, whereas Jesus sees them as thieves and robbers (John 10:1). If every believer dedicated a little time daily to study the 66 canonized books of the Bible systematically, many would self-deliver from these lies. To address this, we have designed an engaging pattern for studying the entire Bible annually in groups with fellow believers. This method encourages sharing thoughts and asking questions from knowledgeable ministers, tackling difficult and long-standing questions of the heart. The presence of other believers ensures consistency and accountability. We recommend choosing a plan based on your hunger for God and time availability, with a minimum goal of completing the Bible once a year. Reading the Bible from beginning to end enriches spiritual life, offering profound insights into God's character, promises, and relationship with His people. Comprehensive Bible reading reveals the overarching narrative of God's love and redemption. We offer plans tailored for different time frames, guiding you through the Scriptures at a pace that suits you, helping you grow spiritually. Follow us and let's enjoy studying the Word of God together, reigniting our commitment to prayer and Bible study, fortifying our faith against deception, and growing in the knowledge and love of God.

Our Vision

In the book of Acts, the local Church met in people's houses and other places convenient for the people. Synagogue was just a place to gather on Sunday to celebrate the risen Lord and because it would be open for public use on the same day as Jews worship on Saturday. The disciples did not need to worry about a physical building before starting a church, because wherever they gathered for fellowship was regarded as the church. With this system, the church grew rapidly because the people are considered as the church not the building. Cell Hubs

Check the list of the cell hubs we have at the moment

Cell Hubs are dynamic discipleship platforms designed to cultivate spiritual growth and community among believers. Each Cell Hub comprises several smaller groups, known as cells, strategically located in various regions. These cells are intimate discipleship networks where members gather regularly to discuss the Word of God, share experiences, and support each other in their spiritual journeys. The primary purpose of Cell Hubs is to create environments where disciples can grow in their faith through fellowship, study, and mutual encouragement. By decentralizing gatherings into smaller, more manageable cells, Cell Hubs ensure that every member receives personal attention and guidance in their spiritual walk. This structure promotes deep, meaningful relationships and fosters a sense of belonging and accountability among members. Each cell within a hub meets regularly, providing a consistent time and place for members to connect, study the Bible, and pray together. These meetings are designed to be interactive and engaging, allowing for open discussions and the sharing of insights and testimonies. Cells are located in various regions, making it convenient for members to join a group close to their home or workplace. This geographical flexibility encourages higher participation and accessibility for everyone interested in joining. THE CORE OF EVERY CELL IS DISCIPLESHIP. Members are encouraged to delve deep into the Scriptures, applying biblical principles to their daily lives. Leaders within each cell provide mentorship and guidance, helping members to grow spiritually and become effective witnesses of their faith. Engaging in regular Bible study and discussions helps individuals to understand and apply God's Word more effectively in their lives. The personalized attention within cells ensures that everyone can grow at their own pace. Cell Hubs foster strong, supportive communities where members can share their struggles, celebrate victories, and pray for one another. This sense of community helps to build lasting friendships and provides a network of support. Being part of a cell provides a level of accountability that is crucial for spiritual growth. Members encourage and challenge each other to live out their faith authentically and responsibly. Cell Hubs are not just inward-focused but also engage in outreach activities. Members are encouraged to invite others to join and to participate in community service and evangelism efforts, spreading the love and message of Christ to a broader audience. We are excited to share the list of cell hubs we have at the moment, each actively nurturing discipleship and community in their respective locations. As we continue to grow, we invite you to join a Cell Hub near you and become part of this transformative movement. By joining a Cell Hub, you can experience the joy of growing in your faith alongside a supportive and loving community. Whether you are new to the faith or looking to deepen your relationship with God, there is a place for you in one of our cell hubs.
